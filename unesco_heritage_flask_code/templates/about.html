About the Project

Understanding our world's most precious cultural and natural treasures through data

{% endblock %} {% block content %}

The UNESCO World Heritage Sites Analysis is a comprehensive data visualization project that explores the distribution, characteristics, and preservation status of the world's most significant cultural and natural landmarks. Using Tableau, this project transforms raw UNESCO data into compelling visual narratives that reveal patterns invisible in spreadsheets alone.

"Heritage is our legacy from the past, what we live with today, and what we pass on to future generations."

The World Heritage Convention, adopted by UNESCO in 1972, represents a landmark international agreement to protect cultural and natural heritage of outstanding universal value. Today, the World Heritage List includes 1,121 sites across 168 countries, spanning five geographic regions and encompassing three distinct categories of heritage.

Project Objectives

🌍

Regional Distribution

Analyze how heritage sites are distributed across UNESCO's five global regions and identify geographic patterns.

⚠️

Endangered Sites

Identify and visualize sites at risk, understanding the threats they face and the urgency of conservation.

📊

Trend Analysis

Examine historical inscription patterns and forecast future trends in heritage site designations.

🏛️

Categorization

Explore the balance between cultural, natural, and mixed heritage sites across different regions.

Data Sources

This analysis draws from multiple authoritative sources to ensure accuracy and comprehensiveness:

  • UNESCO World Heritage Centre — Official database of all inscribed sites
  • UNESCO Statistics — Country and regional designation data
  • Mapbox / OpenStreetMap — Geographic visualization basemaps
  • Historical Inscription Records — Data spanning 1972 to 2026

Methodology

The data was processed and analyzed using Tableau, a leading data visualization platform. Multiple chart types were employed to best represent different aspects of the data:

🗺️

Geographic Maps

Choropleth and point maps for regional and country-level analysis

📈

Time Series

Line charts with forecasting for temporal trend analysis

🥧

Proportional Charts

Pie charts, treemaps, and bubble charts for composition analysis

📊

Bar & Dual-Axis

Comparative bar charts and dual-axis combinations for complex metrics
